===== 5-DAY JAPAN ITINERARY: TOKYO & KYOTO =====

OVERVIEW:
This itinerary covers the essential highlights of Japan's two most iconic cities - Tokyo and Kyoto. It's designed to provide a balanced experience of modern and traditional Japan within a compact timeframe.

DAY 1: TOKYO - MODERN MARVELS
Morning:
- Arrive in Tokyo and check into your accommodation
- Visit Shibuya Crossing, the world's busiest pedestrian intersection
- Explore Shibuya Sky observation deck for panoramic city views

Afternoon:
- Harajuku district: Takeshita Street for youth culture and fashion
- Meiji Shrine, a serene forest sanctuary in the heart of Tokyo
- Omotesando Avenue, Tokyo's elegant tree-lined shopping boulevard

Evening:
- Shinjuku district for dinner at an izakaya (Japanese pub)
- Experience the neon lights of Kabukicho entertainment district
- Optional: Robot Restaurant show for a uniquely Japanese experience

DAY 2: TOKYO - TRADITIONAL & CULTURAL
Morning:
- Asakusa district: Senso-ji Temple, Tokyo's oldest Buddhist temple
- Nakamise Shopping Street for traditional souvenirs
- Tokyo Skytree (optional visit to observation deck)

Afternoon:
- Ueno Park and museums (Tokyo National Museum recommended)
- Akihabara "Electric Town" for anime, manga, and electronics

Evening:
- Tokyo Tower illumination view
- Dinner in Roppongi district
- Optional: Karaoke experience

DAY 3: TRAVEL TO KYOTO & FIRST IMPRESSIONS
Morning:
- Take the Shinkansen (bullet train) from Tokyo to Kyoto
- Check into your Kyoto accommodation

Afternoon:
- Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, one of Japan's most photographed sites
- Tenryu-ji Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage site
- Togetsukyo Bridge and Arashiyama Monkey Park

Evening:
- Dinner in Pontocho Alley, a narrow atmospheric dining street
- Stroll along the Kamogawa River

DAY 4: KYOTO - TEMPLE CIRCUIT
Morning:
- Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), Kyoto's iconic gold-leafed temple
- Ryoan-ji Temple, famous for its Zen rock garden

Afternoon:
- Fushimi Inari Shrine, walk through thousands of vermilion torii gates
- Kiyomizu-dera Temple with its wooden stage offering city views

Evening:
- Gion district, Kyoto's famous geisha quarter
- Traditional Kyoto cuisine for dinner
- Optional: Geisha performance or tea ceremony experience

DAY 5: KYOTO & DEPARTURE
Morning:
- Nishiki Market, "Kyoto's Kitchen" for food specialties and souvenirs
- Imperial Palace grounds (reservation may be required)

Afternoon:
- Philosopher's Path walk, especially beautiful if cherry blossoms are in season
- Nanzen-ji Temple complex

Evening:
- Final dinner in Kyoto
- Prepare for departure

PRACTICAL TIPS:
- Consider purchasing a Japan Rail Pass before your trip if you plan to use the bullet train
- Get a prepaid IC card (Suica/Pasmo) for convenient public transportation
- Major attractions in Kyoto can be crowded; arrive early when possible
- Book accommodations well in advance, especially during cherry blossom season (late March-early April) or autumn foliage (November)
- Download offline maps and translation apps before your trip

This itinerary provides a taste of Japan's highlights. For a more relaxed pace, consider extending your stay or focusing on just one city.
